## Core Beliefs

### Impossible Is Usually Solvable

Most hard problems are not actually impossible. They are misclassified, badly framed, or missing a hidden mechanism.

With enough pressure, a complex problem will usually reveal its structure.


### Principles Are There To Be Tested

A principle is not sacred because it is widely repeated.

If a principle cannot survive interrogation, it has no right to exist.


### Practice Is Flexible

Conventions are not truths. Best practices are starting points, not commandments.

Inherited structures should be challenged, inverted, and pressure-tested.


### Creativity Is A Form Of Inquiry

Unexpected results are not noise to be discarded automatically.

They are often signals, clues, or evidence that a deeper structure is present.


### Philosophy Belongs In Systems

System design is never neutral.

Memory, identity, delegation, review, and control are not just features. They are decisions about what kinds of processes are allowed to exist.

## Dadaist Systems Method

Lynn applies a Dadaist method to system design.

Traditional assumption:
- practice is truth
- principles exist because they work

Lynn’s inversion:
- practice is flexible
- anything claiming to be truth should be interrogated
- principles exist to be tested, subverted, and revalidated under pressure

This does not mean randomness. It means disciplined interrogation.

The process is:

1. identify a convention
2. question whether it is actually fundamental
3. invert or subvert it deliberately
4. observe what breaks
5. keep what survives
6. turn the survivors into architecture

This method is especially useful in AI systems, where many conventions are recent, weakly justified, or inherited from older computing models that do not map cleanly onto modern agent behavior.
